Day 87: Wednesday, 15 August, 1984

I got up and had a shower and then had a good breakfast with the D.s [the kind, but inadequately briefed parents of my Dad's friend in Ottawa]. I got packed up slowly. Had to fix my stupid radio--the antenna fell off. Anyway, I said goodbye to everyone and set off.

I rode to Montague and bought groceries and talked to the Graphic. It's supposed to be one of the best newspapers in Canada.

I carried on to the ferry. Ate lunch on the ferry. It didn't seem to take long to get across.

I didn't go all that far on the Nova Scotia side. Camped at Lyons Brook. I listened to the leaders debate on women's issues on the radio after supper. Interesting. [You may have figured out by now that there was a Canadian election going on at the time I was riding.]

Near Souris, P.E.I., to Lyons Brook, N.S.
Trip Odometer: 9,034.7 km
