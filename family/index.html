You may have a number of questions about Urban Shipleys. Perhaps they will be answered below in our IAQs (Infrequently Asked Questions):

What is an Urban Shipley? The Urban Shipleys came into being when Dave Shipley and Ruth Urban got married. The compound name "Urban Shipley" contains a hidden source of conflict. Shipley is an old Yorkshire name that means "a shelter for sheep" (Dave's ancestors were apparently sheep thieves), but Urban means "of or pertaining to the city." Therefore, our name means "urban sheep shed." In most municipalities, we are therefore a zoning violation. Oh, the humanity!
How long have there been Urban Shipleys? The Urban Shipley combination was created on 1 August 1992. Ruth and Dave celebrated their thirtieth anniversary on 1 August 2022. Dave thinks those charts that tell you what kind of gift you're supposed to buy for which anniversary are kind of silly, so he likes to use the Periodic Table. According to that theory, the thirtieth was the Zinc Anniversary. For more information on zinc, please feel free to consult this fine on-line Periodic Table. Gifts and celebrations can get interesting with this system. Carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen were easy - carbon-based life forms all involve those elements, so Dave gave Ruth chocolates. To celebrate our Fluorine Anniversary, we went out for a nice dinner . . . and brushed our teeth afterwards. Since then, we've gotten a bit lax. To celebrate neon, we failed to go to Vegas. Dave was a bit more successful with the Sodium Anniversary: he found some salt with the brand name "Our Very Own." It was very romantic. For the Magnesium Anniversary, we failed to get new mag wheels for the station wagon. Our plans for aluminum also got foiled. The Silicon Anniversary was going to be easy: Dave was going to re-do the caulking around the bathtub. But he didn't. For Argon we could have replaced our windows, but we had already done so years earlier. For calcium, I think we had some ice cream. Zinc should have really galvanized us into action, but we didn't really do anything unusual. We're not that great at this.
